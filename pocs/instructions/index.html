Set up your XenReality Pi

A simple, step-by-step guide to connect your Raspberry Pi 5 with just the power adapter and one LAN cable. Click Next after each step until you're done.

What is a Raspberry Pi?

A Raspberry Pi is a tiny computer about the size of a credit card. Inside your store it works as the on-site brain for XenReality: it connects to your camera system, understands what's happening in the store, and sends insights to your dashboard.

It only needs two connections to do its job:

  • Power: the USB-C adapter that comes in the box.
  • Network: one LAN cable to your router or switch.

No screens, no keyboards, no extra wiring. If you can plug in a phone charger, you can set this up.

F&B Step 1 of 9

Check your kit

Lay everything out on a clean table. You should have:

  • Raspberry Pi 5 board
  • Official Pi 5 case (red base, white frame with fan, white lid, heatsink & feet in a small envelope)
  • 27W USB-C power adapter (comes with the Pi)
  • LAN cable
  • SD card provided by XenReality
Having a problem?

Something missing? Don't start the setup with a partial kit. Contact your XenReality point of contact and we'll ship the missing part.

Case, part 1: seat the Pi in the red base

  1. Open the accessories envelope in the case box.
  2. Peel the backing off the small heatsink and stick it onto the raised CPU (the silver square in the middle of the board). Press down firmly.
  3. Stick the four silicone feet into the marked spots under the red base.
  4. Lower the Pi into the red base. The board slides under the plastic tab at the SD-card end. Check it sits flat and every port lines up with its hole.
Having a problem?

Board won't sit flat? It's probably not tucked under the tab at the SD-card end. Lift it out, slide the SD-card end in first at an angle, then lower the port side down.

Ports don't line up? The board is in backwards. The USB/Ethernet ports must face the large openings.

Case, part 2: fan cable, frame & lid

  1. Lay the white frame (the part with the fan) upside-down next to the base.
  2. Plug the fan cable into the small 4-pin connector labeled FAN on the Pi. The connector only fits one way, so don't force it.
  3. Clip the white frame onto the red base until it clicks all around.
  4. Press the lid on top. Done! The Pi is now safe inside its case.
Having a problem?

Fan cable won't go in? Flip the connector around: it's keyed to fit only one way. Make sure it's pushed fully in.

Frame won't clip shut? The fan cable may be pinched, or the board isn't flat in the base. Re-check step 2 before pressing harder.

Insert the XenReality SD card

The SD card is the Pi's memory. All the XenReality software is already on it.

  1. Find the thin SD slot on the underside of the case, at the end with the small opening.
  2. Slide the card in with the gold contacts facing up towards the board, until it stops.
Having a problem?

Card won't go in? Don't force it. Flip it over: the card only seats fully in one orientation.

No SD card in the kit? Stop here and contact XenReality. The Pi cannot run without our card.

Connect the power adapter

  1. Plug the adapter into a wall socket, but keep the wall switch OFF for now.
  2. Plug the USB-C end into the Pi's power port: the small oval port on the side of the case (circled in the picture).

Use only the 27W adapter that came with the Pi. Phone chargers may look identical but can't power it reliably.

Having a problem?

Can't find the power port? It's the small oval (USB-C) port, the same shape as a modern phone charging port, on the side edge of the case next to the two HDMI ports.

Connect the LAN cable

  1. Click one end of the LAN cable into the Ethernet port on the Pi's case (the wide square port).
  2. Click the other end into a free port on the store's router or network switch.

This one cable is the Pi's entire connection to the world, so it must go to the same network as the store's cameras.

Having a problem?

No free port on the router? Ask the store's IT contact for a free port on a switch. Do not unplug existing cables yourself.

Cable too short? Any standard Ethernet cable works, but the Pi should sit somewhere safe, dry and ventilated, so prefer moving the Pi closer over stretching cables across walkways.

Power on and check the lights

  1. Turn the wall switch on.
  2. Press the power button on the case.
  3. Look at the Ethernet port: within a minute its little LEDs should light up and blink. Blinking = the Pi is talking to the network.
Having a problem?

No lights at all on the Pi? It's a power problem: check the wall switch, that the USB-C plug is fully in, and that the socket itself works (test with a phone charger).

Pi is on but LAN LEDs never blink? It's a network problem: re-seat both ends of the LAN cable until they click, try a different router/switch port, and check the router itself is powered.

Connect to the NVR

The NVR is the box that records all the cameras. Our Pi reads from it. All you do now is open it and log in:

  1. Ask the store's IT contact for the Pi's address (or find it yourself with the free Fing app: look for "Raspberry Pi" in the list).
  2. On a laptop or phone on the store's network, type that address into the browser.
  3. Log in with the username and password from XenReality.
Having a problem?

What is this "address"? Every device on a network gets its own number, called an IP address. It looks like 192.168.1.45. Think of it as the Pi's apartment number inside the building.

Page won't open? Make sure your laptop/phone is on the same network as the Pi (not a guest WiFi), and re-check the address. If the Pi's LAN LEDs aren't blinking, go back to step 7 first.

Only have a "guest" login? A guest account can watch cameras but can't enable remote view. Ask the client (or their IT contact) for an admin account before continuing.

Turn on Remote View

Remote view lets XenReality securely see the system from outside the store, so our team can monitor and support it without a site visit.

  1. In the NVR you just logged into, switch on Remote View in the settings.
  2. Check the camera view loads. That's it.
Having a problem?

Works in-store but Remote View won't load? The store's firewall is probably blocking the Pi. A firewall is like a security guard at the network's door, checking all traffic. Sometimes it's strict enough to block us, and Fortinet (FortiGate) is the one we see most.

The fix: whitelist the Pi (needs the client's IT admin).

  1. Log into the FortiGate admin console.
  2. Policy & Objects → Addresses → add an address object with the Pi's IP (and whitelist the Pi's MAC ID).
  3. Policy & Objects → Firewall Policy → allow traffic to/from that address (XenReality can confirm the exact ports).
  4. Save, apply, and try Remote View again.

NVR & Tech Requirements: Summary

The client must have all of this ready before installation. Full interactive checklist: the Requirements page.

Cameras

  • ONVIF compatible, on the Local Area Network, with an IP address each
  • Min 2 MP (4–8 MP preferred), dome or bullet
  • Username & password per camera
  • All brand AI features OFF (no person/face detection, motion capture or bounding boxes)
  • Repositioning allowed; floor plan with camera positions provided
  • Supported brands: CP Plus, Hikvision, Dahua, Univision, Bosch, Sony, Axis, Hanwha Vision, Intelbras, Uniview

NVR

  • ONVIF compatible (a MUST); same brand as cameras preferred
  • Same Local Area Network as the Pi
  • Username & password provided; all brand AI features OFF
  • Backup and video storage configured beforehand
  • No NVR access? Client must provide 15–20 hours of store traffic footage instead

Infrastructure

  • Internet at least 50 Mbps; power backup
  • Ethernet port on the LAN + cable channels to run the cable
  • Secure storage room for the Pi
  • Router access (username & password); static IP for cameras and DVR/NVR
  • Firewall (Fortinet): allow LAN access and whitelist the Pi's MAC ID

Access

  • Admin access only: a guest account cannot enable remote view
  • If any requirement fails, inform the XenReality Point of Contact, the Project Owner and the client's technical team immediately
